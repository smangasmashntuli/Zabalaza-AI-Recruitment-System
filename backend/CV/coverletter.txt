A cover letter is a one-page document that introduces you to an employer, highlights your relevant skills and achievements, and explains why you are a strong fit for a specific role.
What is a Cover Letter
A cover letter is typically submitted alongside your resume as part of a job application. It allows you to expand on your experience, showcase your personality, and explain why you are interested in the position and company. Unlike a resume, which lists qualifications, a cover letter provides context and demonstrates your communication skills and cultural fit for the organization.

Structure of a Cover Letter
Header: Include your name, address, phone number, and email, followed by the employer’s contact information.
Greeting: Address the letter to a specific person whenever possible, using "Dear [Name]" or "Dear [Position Title]".
Opening Paragraph: State the job title you are applying for, where you found the posting, and briefly explain your interest in the role and company.
Body Paragraph(s): Highlight your relevant skills, achievements, and experiences. Use specific examples to demonstrate how you can add value to the organization.
Closing Paragraph: Reiterate your interest, express enthusiasm for an interview, and thank the reader for their time.
Signature: End with a professional closing such as "Sincerely," followed by your name.

Tips for Writing an Effective Cover Letter
Tailor each letter to the specific job and company to show genuine interest.
Use measurable achievements to demonstrate impact, such as "increased social media engagement by 50%".
Keep it concise: Typically three to four paragraphs, one page maximum.
Show personality while maintaining professionalism.
Proofread carefully to avoid errors and ensure clarity.
